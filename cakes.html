Apple crumble cake

300g apples
100ml oil
175g light brown sugar
0.5tbsp apple cider vinegar
1/2 tbsp vanilla
240ml milk
250g plain flour
2 tsp cinnamon
0.5tsp ground cardamom
0.75 tsp BofS
1.5 tsp BP

Topping:
56g plain flour
31g sugar
44g butter

160°
45-50mins
8” round

Method

  1. Make crumble topping and set aside.
  2. Cut apples into small pieces, about 0.75cm.
  3. Combine oil, vinegar and vanilla.
  4. Gradually mix in sugar.
  5. Add in milk and combine.
  6. Sift dry over wet in 2-3 batches.
  7. Stir in apples, top with crumble and bake.

Chocolate and caramel cake

47.5g cocoa
120g light brown sugar
1tsp vanilla
87.5ml just boiled water
210g P flour
1/2 tsp BP
1/2 tsp B of S
125g butter
140 sugar
2 eggs

Filling:
150g sugar
150ml double cream
1/2 tsp vanilla
1tsp sea salt

Topping:
110g egg white (<3 eggs)
160g sugar
235g butter

160°
20-25mins
2 x 8” round

Method

  1. Whisk cocoa, sugar, vanilla and water until smooth and leave cool 5 mins.
  2. Cream butter and sugar, 3-5 mins, then beat in eggs 1 at a time.
  3. Fold in dry 1/3 at a time, then cooled cocoa mixture.
  4. Bake.
  5. For caramel, sugar and 6tbsp water in saucepan over medium heat, cook 6-8 mins no stirring, until starts to turn deep amber.
  6. Remove from heat and place in bowl cold water.
  7. Slowly whisk in cream and vanilla -> thick pouring consistency (can stir over heat if necessary).
  8. Stir in salt and leave to cool.
  9. For buttercream, whisk egg whites and sugar until smooth.
  10. Place over pan of simmering water for 5mins, whisking occasionally until sugar dissolves - feels smooth, not grainy between fingertips.
  11. Whisk about 7mins to stiff peaks.
  12. Add butter and whisk about 5mins, until smooth and glossy - don’t panic if appears to curdle, just keep whisking.
  13. Add vanilla and 5 tbsp cooled caramel, 1tbsp at a time and mix until smooth.

Notes

  • Not too chocolately or too sweet.
  • Buttercream very smooth and silky. Could do with less buttercream.
  • Caramel sauce did not work as stated - hard lumps of sugar and had to heat slowly to get mix with cream - next time would not dunk in cold water, but would add warm cream instead.

Devil’s food cake

2oz cocoa
6floz boiling water
6oz marg
12oz dark brown sugar
3 eggs
10oz P flour
3/2tsp B of S
1/4 tsp BP
4floz soured cream/buttermilk

180°
30-35mins
9” round

Method

  1. Mix and cool cocoa and water.
  2. Cream marg, sugar and eggs.
  3. Sift dry twice.
  4. Add dry to creamed in 3 batches, alternating with dairy.

Notes

  • Comes out a tall 2x8”, think would be better to have 3x8”.
  • 150g cream/choc was enough ganache to fill and top 2x8”, but not sides.

Kladdkaka

150ml aquafaba
180g sugar
150g P flour
40g cocoa
2tsp BP
1/4tsp salt
100ml oil
icing sugar

200°
15-20mins
8” round

Method

  1. Whisk aquafaba and sugar to white and fluffy (~5min, speed 4).
  2. Combine dry ingredients, then gently fold in aquafaba mix.
  3. Add oil and stir to smooth.
  4. Bake until edges are firm and dry to touch, middle will be wet.
  5. Cool in tin, dust.

Notes

  • Recipe said 13-15mins, but gave it 20. Could maybe have had a couple longer whilst still being fudgy in middle, but unsure, maybe oven not right temperature.

Lemon cake

295ml milk
10ml apple cider vinegar
120ml aquafaba
115g butter
20ml oil
300g sugar
1 tbsp lemon zest
1 tbsp lemon extract
1dsp vanilla
350g P flour
12g baking powder
3g salt
turmeric

175°
?mins
2 x 8” round

Method

  1. Combine milk and vinegar and sit aside for buttermilk.
  2. Mix aquafaba 1-2 mins high speed into light and foamy and set aside.
  3. Beat butter, oil, sugar and lemon zest 3 mins until light and fluffy.
  4. Mix in buttermilk and aquafaba.
  5. Fold in rest, do not overmix.

Notes

  • Paired with buttercream with lemon zest.
